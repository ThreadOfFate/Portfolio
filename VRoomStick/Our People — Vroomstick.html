OUR PEOPLE

 

Deborah Drury spent 14 years working in full-time education from 2001 - 2015. She graduated with distinction in Educational Management (MEd) at Queen's University in 2009 before joining Keith Drury Art as joint Company Director in 2015. Deborah founded VRoomstick in 2019 during the final stages of completing a doctoral degree programme which is focusing on investigating Virtual Reality and Autistic Spectrum Disorder. Known as ‘Debs’ to her friends, she enjoys travel, reading and spending time with Harvey her golden cocker spaniel.

Matthew McComb initially joined VRoomstick part-time as an undergraduate student. During this period, his expertise in Virtual Reality proved invaluable to the team’s projects. Matthew subsequently became a full-time staff member in 2018 after graduating from QUB with a MSc degree in Computer Games Development. In his spare time, Matthew enjoys table-top and video games, as well as spending long hours watching his favourite shows on Netflix.

Keith Drury is a well-known Northern Irish artist. A business graduate and former board member of Belfast City Centre Management, Keith’s most recent artworks are vibrant interpretations of cityscapes created by 3D modelling. Beyond commercial work, Keith also creates large-scale oil paintings which act as social commentaries. These pieces have featured on the BBC, UTV, The Daily Mail, The Irish Times, The Observer and Private Eye. Keith's art can be viewed at www.keithdruryart.com As VR requires 3D models to navigate scenes in 3D space, Keith’s sculpting skills are well employed at VRoomstick. Unsurprisingly, his hobbies mainly revolve around all things artistic, but Keith can also make the world’s best pancakes if persuaded to do so!

Keith and Deborah are honoured to be Patrons of the National Autistic Society in Northern Ireland.
